Understanding Utility

1 Cardinal Utility

Utility that can be measured in exact numbers. For example, "This banana gives me 10 utils of satisfaction."

🍌

Example: Banana = 10 utils

Measurable satisfaction

2 Ordinal Utility

Utility expressed as preferences or rankings. "I prefer bananas to apples" without exact numbers.

>
🍎 Apple

3 Total, Average & Marginal Utility

Total Utility (TU)

Total satisfaction from all units consumed

Average Utility (AU)

TU divided by quantity consumed

Marginal Utility (MU)

Extra satisfaction from one more unit

Core Concepts

1 Total Product (TP)

The total output produced by all units of a variable factor (like labor) with fixed factors (like capital).

Formula: TP = Q (Total Quantity of Output)

2 Average Product (AP)

The output per unit of variable factor. It shows productivity per worker or per unit of input.

Formula: AP = TP / Number of Workers (or Variable Input)

3 Marginal Product (MP)

The additional output produced by adding one more unit of variable factor.

Formula: MP = Change in TP / Change in Variable Input
